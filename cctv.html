HD CCTV


High Definition  CCTV.
DTEC Systems presents High Definition Composite Video Interface (HDCVI) technology to the market. HDCVI is an over-coaxial-cable analog HD video transmission standard, allowing long-distance HD transmission at a lower cost to meet the requirements for complex and large installations.Analog systems, based on coaxial cabling, still dominate today’s video surveillance market.The demand for high definition from end-users is undoubtedly increasing; however, the need to change the cabling for a HD system can result in higher operational costs. With no new cabling or IP hassle required, HDCVI is one option for upgrading existing systems from standard definition to high definition, offering longer transmission via coaxial cable and lower total cost....

IPTV

Internet Protocol Television(IPTV)
IPTV is a digitized and networked version of CCTV.In an IP surveillance system,IP cameras record video footage and the resulting content is distributed over an IP network.Digitization offers a number of benefits over traditional analogue CCTV,including:Improves search capability,Greater ease of use,Better image quality and no degradation of content over time,The ability to record and play simultaneously,The ability to compress content for improved storage.Adding network capability to digital CCTV provides additional benefits,including:Improved ability for remote viewing and control.Anyone on the network can potentially see video footage from any camera connected to the IP network.IP storage makes it possible to store data in any geographical location.Greater ease of distribution means an image of crime suspect can be immediately distributed to officials.There is a growing industry trend towards replacing CCTV with IPTV
